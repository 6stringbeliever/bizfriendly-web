BizFriendly is a project created in collaboration with Kansas City, Missouri, the Unified Government of Wyandotte County and Kansas City, Kansas and the 2013 Code for America Fellows.

What’s BizFriendly?

BizFriendly helps you put the web to work for your business.

Who’s It For?

BizFriendly is built for small business owners who are

  • focused on trying to run a business and don’t have time to find new tech
  • find learning tech too hard or time consuming
  • wanting to find out what tools other businesses use & how you compare

What does it do?

Find new tools With the latest web tools in one place, you can easily find what to use and start learning to make your business even better. Check them out! Learn how to use those tools You’ll quickly learn through lessons that guide you step-by-step, track your progress, and provide feedback along the way. Try one out! Connect & compare to others Soon you’ll be able to join a community of learners to connect and see how you compare. How is the content created? To keep the content fresh, teachers and web services can easily create new lessons through an integrated lesson builder. Check out the lesson builder prototype. Why are we building it? Charged with the topic of economic development, the Code for America fellows conducted a research and ideation process and discovered a need for improving digital literacy in Kansas City as means to economic development. Kansas City has an amazing business community with over 48,000 small businesses. That’s a 26th-place ranking among the nation’s 938 metropolitan and micropolitan areas! Kansas City also has a digital advantage by becoming one of the most Internet-connected cities in the country through Google Fiber and other local initiatives, such as Connecting for Good. But amongst Kansas Citians, there’s a drop off in web skills beyond the very basics. There’s room for improvement to to make Kansas City small business owners the most digitally literate in the country and BizFriendly will help achieve that.
